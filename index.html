Endocrine System

What is the Endocrine System?

The Endocrine System contains glands. Glands are organs that secrete chemicals called hormones. Endocrine glands are ductless glands which secrete hormones into the bloodstream. Once the hormones are released, they go to any part of the body which is supplied by blood.

Endocrine organs and their functions:

  • Adrenal Glands: keep the control of preparing the body for action. It is a chemical messenger and its actions are very rapid but only for a short time. It causes: skin to become pale; faster breething and fast heartbeat
  • Ovaries: Oestrogen and Progesterone control puberty in females which include development of breasts and hips. It also controls the menstrual cycle and ovulation.
  • Testes: Testosterone controls puberty in males which include deepening the voice and growth of body hair. It also controls the development and release of sperm.
  • Pancreas: controls blood sugar concetration with Insulin and Glucagon. Insulin is released when blood sugar is too high and Glucagon when blood sugar is too low.

What is feedback?

Feedback is the organ which controls the production of hormones. When the hormone level gets higher, it inhibits its own production for the level never to get to high and when the hormone in the blood falls, it stimulates its own production so that the level never gets too low. Feedback Control is important in homeostatis.
